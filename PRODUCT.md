# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single power user: Tom, owner of Bolsover Contracting (Dubai), running the entire business from a desktop/laptop at a desk. He lives in this app daily — moving between enquiries, RFQs, quotes, projects, invoices, and scheduling. The job to be done is running a construction contracting business end-to-end without drowning in admin: classify incoming enquiries, get supplier pricing, send quotes, win work, invoice it.

## Product Purpose

BolsoverOS is a self-built construction management platform ("business operating system") replacing scattered spreadsheets and email threads. It covers the full commercial pipeline: enquiries → RFQs to suppliers → quotes to clients (with DocuSign acceptance) → projects, variations, sitework, subcontractors → invoices (Zoho Books sync). Success means Tom can process an enquiry to a sent quote in minutes and always knows the state of every job at a glance.

## Brand Personality

Fun, contemporary, design-driven. This is a tool built by its own user, so it should be a pleasure to open — personality in the details, not corporate blandness. Friendly and spacious in the spirit of Notion/Craft: content-first, breathing room, warmth in micro-interactions. It should feel like a modern product someone chose to craft, not software the business was forced to buy.

## Anti-references

- **Boring enterprise software**: gray SAP/Oracle-style form walls, cramped tables, bureaucratic density.
- **Generic SaaS admin template**: identical stat-card grids, purple gradients, stock dashboard clichés.
- The fun should never tip into toy-like — it still runs a real contracting business with real money.

## Design Principles

1. **One person's cockpit** — every screen optimizes for a single expert user who knows the domain; no onboarding hand-holding, but state must always be legible at a glance (pipeline stage, what needs action next).
2. **Minutes, not clicks** — the enquiry→quote workflow is the heartbeat; reduce steps, surface the next action, keep forms fast.
3. **Personality in the details** — delight lives in micro-interactions, empty states, and copy, not in decoration that slows work down.
4. **Content-first spaciousness** — Notion/Craft-style breathing room; data density where it earns its place (tables, schedules), calm everywhere else.
5. **Own the desktop-OS metaphor** — the app-shell/windowed identity is the product's signature; refine it rather than flattening into a generic dashboard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard good practice: WCAG AA contrast throughout (body text ≥4.5:1), full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation. No known special user needs.
